Indian weddings are more than mere celebrations; they are once-in-a-lifetime experiences filled with love, laughter, fun, thrill and emotions. This special day in every woman’s life becomes more special when she walks down the aisle in her favourite bridal lehenga made specially for her. Lehenga is a traditional Indian outfit for women that consists of a long, flowy, & voluminous skirt, a sleek fit blouse & a trendy yet traditional dupatta. Lehengas are often made in the most luxurious fabrics in India including silk, velvet, net, georgette, chiffon and many more. Further, the heavy bridal lehengas are usually embellished with zari and threadwork. Stones, beads, mirrors and sequins turn these lehengas into the most magical outfits.

Which Are The Trending Bridal Wedding Lehenga Styles?

Wedding fashion is celebrated in India. Designers work day and night to create the best wedding outfits for Indian couples to look mesmerising at their wedding ceremony. The Dulhan lehenga is one of the most talked about wedding outfits. After all, have we all not dreamt about our wedding lehenga forever? While we have all been dreaming about it, different women have different preferences when it comes to their lehenga. Look at the most popular styles from simple bridal lehenga to traditional ones.

Rajasthani Lehenga

1.Traditional red with mirror work and gota patti

2.Bandhani dupatta with heavy border

3.Kundan and stone embellished blouse

Gujarati Lehenga

1.Panetar colour combination (white and red)

2.Heavy gharchola work

3.Traditional coin jewellery pairing

South Indian Lehenga

1.Silk lehenga with temple border

Traditional Lehenga

Floral Lehenga

Sequin Lehenga

Velvet Lehenga

Pastel Lehenga

Georgette Lehenga

Zardozi Lehenga

What Are The Latest Wedding Lehenga Designs For This Wedding Season?

With the wedding season in full swing, brides-to-be are on the hunt for the perfect lehenga to dazzle on their special day. This year, trends are moving beyond the traditional red and embracing an array of colours, from blush pink and mint green to bold royal blue. Minimal bridal lehenga-new design is also taking centre stage, with intricate threadwork and subtle embellishments replacing heavy embroidery. The A-line dulhan lehenga design remains a popular choice for a flattering silhouette, while the lehenga-saree fusion offers a unique and unconventional twist. And for the eco-conscious bride, sustainable fabrics like organic silk and khadi are making a statement. Remember, the most important thing is to choose a lehenga that makes you feel confident and radiantly beautiful – after all, it's your day to shine!

Which Colour Is Trending Now In Bridal Lehenga?

However, modern brides are experimenting with unique colours and trends like pastel colours and muted tones. While keeping the trends in mind is a good thing, be sure that you aren’t following trends blindly while choosing your bridal lehenga. How Much Do Wedding Lehengas Cost?

The cost incurred in your bridal lehenga depends on several factors. To start with, the fabric used to create the lehenga and the intricacy of the work decides the price of the lehenga. Further, the designer label matters a lot regarding your lehenga’s cost. In India, you can get a bridal lehenga for as little as ₹15,000 from a local market, and the prices can go up to ₹8,00,000- ₹10,00,000 depending on the designer. How soon should you start shopping for Bridal Lehengas?

Ideally, 6 to 9 months. Your wedding is a once-in-lifetime event and you got to look perfect on this special day. You must start looking for your options at least 6-9 months before the wedding day. This can be done by keeping an eye on bridal fashion shows, visiting designer stores, and browsing the latest bridal lehnga for wedding trends in Weddings By Betterhalf’s ideabook. 4-6 months before your wedding is the time when you must have a narrowed-down list of options for your bridal couture. 2-3 months before your wedding is the time to decide on your ultimate lehenga for wedding. The stitching of your lehenga must be done a month before your wedding. Remember to try your outfit a week before your wedding to make sure it fits.

How to Choose the Perfect Bridal Lehenga

A beautiful bridal lehenga is a blend of personal style, comfort, and cultural aesthetics. Starting the search early allows for a thoughtful exploration of various designs and styles. Setting a budget is key, ensuring that the dream lehenga aligns with financial considerations. Consider your body type and opt for a silhouette that enhances your natural beauty. The choice of fabric, whether silk, velvet, or georgette, should harmonize with the wedding season. While traditional reds and maroons are timeless, contemporary brides are embracing a spectrum of colours, letting personal preferences and the overall theme guide their choice. Comfort is paramount for a day filled with festivities, and a trial is essential to assess fit and movement. Trusting your instincts in the selection process ensures that the chosen bridal lehenga not only complements the celebration's aesthetics but also becomes a cherished reflection of your unique style and the joy of the occasion.
